The Story Behind the Name

Etymology

The province of Sultan Kudarat got its name from Sultan Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat, one of the most powerful and respected Muslim leaders in the history of Mindanao.

Sultan Kudarat began to rise as a leader in the early 17th century and formally ruled from 1625 to 1671. During his reign, he united various groups in Mindanao and led strong resistance against Spanish forces.

Because of his bravery, intelligence, and leadership, he successfully prevented the Spanish from fully colonizing parts of Mindanao, especially the Cotabato region.

Sultan Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat is recognized as a national hero in the Philippines. In honor of his legacy, the province was named after him when it was officially created in 1973.

Today, the name "Sultan Kudarat" represents strength, unity, and cultural pride.

Did you know? Sultan Kudarat is considered one of the greatest Muslim heroes in Philippine history for resisting Spanish colonization.

  • Full Name: Sultan Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat
  • Reign: 1625 – 1671
  • Region: Mindanao
  • Known for: Resistance against Spanish colonization
